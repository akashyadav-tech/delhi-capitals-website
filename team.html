Our History

The Delhi Capitals, formerly known as Delhi Daredevils, is a professional franchise cricket team based in Delhi that plays in the Indian Premier League (IPL). Founded in 2008, the team is co-owned by the GMR Group and the JSW Group.

Under the leadership of captain Rishabh Pant and head coach Ricky Ponting, Delhi Capitals have emerged as one of the most exciting teams in the IPL, reaching the finals in 2020 and consistently performing at the highest level.

The franchise rebranded to Delhi Capitals in December 2018, symbolizing the capital city of India and its status as the center of power, culture, and youth. The change reflected the team's new approach - a focus on young talent and fearless cricket.

Our home ground is the Arun Jaitley Stadium in Delhi, where we enjoy massive support from our passionate fans. The team's philosophy is built around nurturing young talent and playing an aggressive, fearless brand of cricket.

Our Home Ground

Arun Jaitley Stadium

Arun Jaitley Stadium, Delhi

Formerly known as Feroz Shah Kotla Stadium, the Arun Jaitley Stadium is our fortress. With a capacity of 41,000 spectators, it's one of the oldest cricket stadiums in India, established in 1883.

The stadium has witnessed some of the most memorable moments in Indian cricket history and is known for its lively pitch that offers assistance to both batsmen and bowlers.

Our home matches at the Arun Jaitley Stadium are always special, with the Delhi crowd creating an electrifying atmosphere that motivates our players to perform at their best.
